About

A biologist who codes.
A coder who thinks biologically.

I have an M.Sc. in Genomic Science and work at the intersection of computational biology, evolutionary genomics, and microbial systems analysis. My work spans comparative genomics, phylogenomics, metagenomics, transcriptomics, and large-scale biological inference, with particular interests in archaeal evolution, genome streamlining, and host-dependent microbial systems.

🧬

Research Identity

I am interested in how evolutionary processes reshape genomes across deep biological timescales. My work focuses on comparative genomics, microbial evolution, genome organisation, and scalable computational approaches for reconstructing evolutionary relationships from complex and heterogeneous biological data.

⚙️

Engineering Philosophy

Reproducibility is non-negotiable. Every workflow I build is modular, documented, version-controlled, and designed for scalable deployment across Linux, HPC, and cloud environments. I believe computational infrastructure should function as a long-term scientific asset rather than disposable analysis code.

📐

Breadth by Design

My computational work spans phylogenomics, metagenomics, transcriptomics, regulatory network inference, and machine learning. The goal is not breadth for its own sake, but developing transferable analytical frameworks capable of addressing biological questions across different genomic scales and systems.

🌍

Currently

Currently developing comparative genome reduction analysis pipelines for archaeal and bacterial symbionts, transcriptome-guided comparative genomics workflows, PANDA/LIONESS regulatory network inference pipelines, and the Kabosu framework for heterogeneous multi-omics integration.

What could I bring to your lab?

If your research involves comparative genomics, phylogenomics, metagenomics, microbial evolution, or large-scale omics integration, we are probably interested in similar computational problems. My work focuses on building reproducible and scalable analytical frameworks for extracting biologically interpretable signals from heterogeneous genomic datasets. I am particularly interested in environments where computational rigour, evolutionary reasoning, and biological interpretation are treated as inseparable parts of the scientific process.
