Research Orientation

I am interested in how evolutionary and population-level processes give rise to genomic variation across complex biological systems. Many biological phenomena can be viewed as dynamic, multi-scale systems in which mutation, selection, drift, and environmental interactions collectively shape the structure of genetic data. From this perspective, genomic patterns are emergent properties of underlying processes rather than isolated signals. My work focuses on developing computational approaches that integrate quantitative models with large-scale sequencing data to infer these generative mechanisms, enabling interpretation that connects statistical structure to biological causation.

Approach

I design reproducible workflows for large-scale datasets using HPC infrastructure and containerized environments. My analyses emphasize explicit assumptions, robustness to noise, and clear interpretability to support reliable downstream research.
