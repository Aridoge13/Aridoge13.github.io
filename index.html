Why conservation genomics?

I believe the motto 'Do Only Good Everyday' must extend to our stewardship of the planet's most vulnerable species. For me, conservation genomics is the practical embodiment of this principle. In the race against extinction, good intentions are not enough—we need precision. This discipline is how we listen carefully to what a species' genome is telling us before we intervene. Building pangenomes to find missing diversity or quantifying genetic load to prevent inbreeding depression—these aren't abstract tasks. They are acts of responsibility and an ethical imperative. Using these high-resolution tools allows us to move beyond guesswork to ensure that our well-intentioned actions, like facilitating connectivity or genetic rescue, are lineage-aware and genuinely good for the species' long-term health. This rigorous, compassionate science is how I strive to ensure our technical skills always serve the highest moral goal: safeguarding the evolutionary future of threatened species.

Approach & readiness

I deliver production-ready code and reproducible HPC workflows (Snakemake + SLURM + containers). My emphasis is on biologically interpretable pipelines that provide transparent outputs for conservation stakeholders. If helpful, I can develop small, interpretable decision-support tools on top of these outputs; these would be explicit downstream aids rather than replacements for biological interpretation.
