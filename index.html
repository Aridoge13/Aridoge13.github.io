Research Focus

I am interested in how evolutionary processes shape cancer progression under immune pressure. Tumors can be viewed as dynamic populations undergoing mutation, selection, and drift, where observable genomic patterns reflect underlying stochastic processes. My goal is to contribute to frameworks that link mechanistic models of tumor evolution with real sequencing data to generate interpretable, biologically meaningful insights.

Approach & readiness

I build reproducible HPC workflows and transparent analytical pipelines designed for large-scale sequencing data. My emphasis is on interpretable outputs, explicit assumptions, and robustness to noise. I am particularly interested in simulation-informed analysis, probabilistic reasoning, and integrating quantitative models with empirical genomic datasets.
