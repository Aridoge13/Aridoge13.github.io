Why conservation genomics — short

I focus on conservation genomics because accurate representations of population-level structural variation and genetic load are essential for effective conservation decisions. Pangenome graphs and long-read assemblies capture diversity that SNP-only approaches miss, and they enable lineage-aware management actions — exactly the outcomes required by the ExPANsion project.

Approach & readiness

I deliver production-ready code and reproducible HPC workflows (Snakemake + SLURM + containers). My emphasis is on biologically interpretable pipelines that provide transparent outputs for conservation stakeholders. If helpful, I can develop small, interpretable decision-support tools on top of these outputs; these would be explicit downstream aids rather than replacements for biological interpretation.
