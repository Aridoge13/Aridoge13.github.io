About

Why archaeal symbionts? Why genome streamlining?

Obligate symbiosis drives some of the most extreme selective pressures in evolution: metabolic outsourcing, progressive genome reduction, and loss of biosynthetic independence. The discovery of DPANN archaea and the Candidate Phyla Radiation showed that this is not an anomaly but a major, understudied dimension of microbial diversity. My work builds scalable computational frameworks to reconstruct these evolutionary trajectories.

🧬

Phylogenomics & dating

I use RAxML, IQ-TREE2, and BEAST to reconstruct evolutionary relationships and estimate divergence times for uncultured symbiont lineages — essential for understanding when host dependence emerged in archaeal evolution.

🔬

Metagenomics & MAGs

Experience processing environmental metagenomes, assembly (metaSPAdes), binning (DASTool), and handling fragmented MAGs from DPANN and other host‑associated lineages — a core challenge in symbiont genomics.

⚙️

Genome reduction pipelines

I built a comparative Genome Dependency Index and PCA‑based reduction landscape for Nanoarchaeum equitans, Asgard archaea, and free‑living relatives. Integrates coding density, hypothetical protein fraction, and metabolic features.

🤖

ML for symbiosis

Developing graph neural networks and network approaches (PANDA/LIONESS) to predict host‑symbiont metabolic complementarity, directly supporting the Vici 2025 machine‑learning integration.
