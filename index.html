About

A biologist who codes.
A coder who thinks biologically.

I have an M.Sc. in Genomic Science and work at the intersection of computational biology, machine learning, and multi-omics data integration. My work spans population genomics, cancer genomics, regulatory network inference, mechanism-aware predictive modelling and single-cell analysis.

🧬

Research Identity

I am interested in how complex biological phenotypes emerge from interacting and context-dependent regulatory processes. My work focuses on modelling these relationships using machine learning, network inference, and multi-modal biological data.

⚙️

Engineering Philosophy

Reproducibility is non-negotiable. Every pipeline I build is modular, documented, version-controlled, and runnable — whether on a laptop or a GCP cluster. I believe that good code is good science.

📐

Breadth by Design

From phylogenomics and metagenomics to GRN inference and deep learning for personalised medicine — my computational breadth is intentional. Systems biology rewards generalists who think in depth.

🌍

Currently

Preparing for doctoral research in computational biology through development of PANDA/LIONESS regulatory network inference pipelines, multi-modal and mechanism-aware modelling approaches for antimicrobial peptide research, and the Kabosu multi-omics integration framework.

What could I bring to your lab?

If your research involves large-scale omics data, network-level biological questions, or integrating heterogeneous datasets, we are probably thinking about similar computational problems. My work spans variant interpretation, regulatory network analysis, single-cell workflows, and machine learning, with a strong emphasis on reproducibility, scalability, and HPC-ready infrastructure. I thrive in environments where biological reasoning and computational rigour are inseparable, and where software is treated as a long-term scientific asset rather than a disposable supplement to publication.
